Stereotype threat

This blog post is suppose to be about how stereotype threats have affected me. I'm a tall, white male from suburban Florida. I have plenty of facial hair, am handsome and intelligent enough to be married to my bright and beautiful wife, and wear a size 12 shoe. I don't think stereotypes affect me. This isn't a way of me 'flexing' my life to you. Its simply my conclusion after considering this possibility for several days.

So, instead, here is my opinion on sterotype threats. They suck. The fear and anxiety of falling into or being part of a stereotype is something I wished no one had to live with. But stereotyping, drawing assumptions on a person based solely off their demographic, has certainly be happening since the dawn of time. I don't imagine stereotyping people is going to stop until we're all just one vanilla shade of unisex weirdness.

So what can be done about it? Modern societies as a whole has been breaking the molds for nearly a century now. And that needs to continue.
