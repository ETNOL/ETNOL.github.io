The challenge topic for this week is to analyze three of my favorite websites for their visual appeal, purpose, and user experience. I began this challenge by doing some research on each of these three aspects. (This research was Powered by Google so take it with a grain of salt.)

The first site that googling "website visual appeal" brought up was this site about...website visual appeal. So I did a quick skim through the article. Two German scientists performed website evaluations and came up with five characteristic pairs that could very accurately predict a website's appeal:

  • interesting-boring
  • good use of color-bad use of color
  • well designed-poorly designed
  • imaginative-unimaginative

What was almost as interesting were the two pairs that did not help predict visual appeal:

  • clear-confusing
  • simple-complex

Another research team found rating a website's appeal could be boiled down to two questions:

  • I found the website to be attractive
  • The website has a clear and simple presentation

However...

the most interesting tidbit I picked up from this article is the appearance and usability of a site has a very strong correlation with a user's trust in the site and product.
